Theory

A Tesseract (or Eight-Cell) is the 4D equivalent of a cube (which has 3 dimensions).
As the surface of the cube consists of six square faces (2-dimensional), the hypersurface of the tesseract consists of eight cubes (3-dimensional).

By our senses, we are limited to 3D, but it's no problem in the mathematical domain : we can perform operations on how many dimensions we want using matrices, then project them back in 2D and display it on a screen !
