Theory

A Tesseract (or Eight-Cell) is the 4D equivalent of a cube (which has 3 dimensions).
As the surface of the cube consists of six square faces (2-dimensional), the hypersurface of the tesseract consists of eight cubes (3-dimensional).

By our senses, we are limited to 3D, but a computer is not. By using 4D matrices, we can rotate the Tesseract on whichever plane we wish, then project it back in 2D to display it on a screen ! We are not even limited to four, there is no limit to the dimensionality of a Hypercube.
