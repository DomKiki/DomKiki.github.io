Projects > Games > Snake

History

Snake is the common name for a video game genre where the player controls a line that grows in length, with the line itself being a primary obstacle. The concept originated in the 1976 arcade game Blockade.
It found a quite larger audience when a variant was preloaded on Nokia mobile phones in 1988. Since then, it has been considered a "classic" and the clone count has grown to such level that there are over 300 Snake-like game for iOS alone.

Rules

  • Going backwards is not possible (e.g pressing Down while going upwards)
  • Food (blue-ish) makes you grow
  • Poison (red) makes you shrink
  • The world is open but hitting a wall or your own tail results in a Game Over
  • Each level the game accelerates and there are more food, poison and walls
  • A portal is granted each 5 completed levels

Controls

  • Directional arrows : control the Snake's direction
  • Spacebar : place portal (two steps)
