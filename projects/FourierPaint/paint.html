Theory

The Discrete Fourier Transform (or DFT) is a mathematical tool in signal processing. It converts a discrete sequence of samples into a complex-valued frequency function of the same length. Said in a simpler way, it decomposes any finite signal into a frequency function, as shown below.

Practice

We'll take any arbitrary signal, here the user's mouse input when he/she drags it on the canvas. We'll then compute the signal's DFT and represent each frequency with a circle orbiting around the previous one (also known as epicycles). Their combined rotations will produce a single output, the DFT's sum, which is the corresponding input point. We can then watch the inputs represented as an epicycloid curve !
