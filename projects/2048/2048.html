History

2048 is a puzzle game created in 2014 by Italian developer Gabriele Cirulli, inpired by Veewo Studio's 1024 which himself is a clone of the game Threes!.
It was described as the Candy Crush for math geeks, also compared to Flappy Bird because of how addictive it is, but without the mindlessness.
Initially released under the MIT License, this game became a viral hit, recieving over 4 million visitors in the first week. Since then, it was ported to almost every platforms and multiple clones were born.

Rules

The game plays out on a blank 4x4 grid consisting with two initialized tiles.
The only move available to the player is sliding the board. Tiles will slide as far as possible until stopped by either another tile or the edge of the grid.
Two tiles with equal values will merge into one when colliding during a move.
The resulting tile will have the total value of the two tiles that collided, and will not be able to merge with another tile before the next move.
Every time the board changes, a new tile spawns in an empty space of the grid, with a 90/10 probability of having a value of 2/4.
The game ends when no tiles can merge and if there are no empty spaces left in the grid.

Controls

  • Directional arrows : Slide the board
