Projects > Maths > Boid Flock

Theory

Craig Reynolds first made this model back in 1986 to simulate animal motion such as bird flocks and fish schools. It consists of three simple steering behaviours to describe how each generic flocking creature (or boid) behaves based on its nearby flockmates, defined by its Field of View or FoV.

Separation from flockmates Alignment with the average heading Cohesion towards the average position

We also added a fourth steering, a repelling force away from the walls, when activated. The closer the boid is to the wall, the greater the force. Not that realistic given that the flocks don't have any destination, still amusing to watch.

Controls

Use the sliders to enhance/diminish the forces.

Press the buttons to en/disable FoV, Velocity and Walls.
